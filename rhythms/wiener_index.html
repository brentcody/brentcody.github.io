These green sets are maximizers of the Wiener index, or equivalently, minimizers of the ``sum of negative distances'' potential energy. The red sets are the "classical" maximally even sets. Try to find musical connections with the green sets that are different from the red sets. For example, the set labeled "12_7_cycle_Wiener_index_12" is maximally even, and it corresponds to the major scale WWHWWWH (W = whole step, H = half step). This set and its rotations correspond to the Heptatonia prima scales, in which the half steps (semi tones) are maximally separated. These are also known as the diatonic modes. Whereas, if we look at a set which is close to "12_7_cycle_Wiener_index_12," like "12_7_cycle_Wiener_index_11," it corresponds to WHWWWWH. This set and its rotations are the Heptatonia secunda scales, in which each half step has one and four whole steps on either side. These are called melodic scalse. The set "12_7_cycle_Wiener_index_6," corresponds to HWWWWWH with two adjacent half steps. This set and its rotations are the Heptatonia tertia scales. The set "12_7_cycle_Wiener_index_10" corresponds to the Harmonic major scale (see picture). What other scales can we find here? What rhythms do these sets correspond to?
